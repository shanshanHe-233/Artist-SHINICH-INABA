BIOGRAPHY

Shinji inaba, Japanese artist. He was born in kagoshima prefecture in 1978. Graduated from Kyoto technical school of traditional crafts in 2000. Shimizu (also known as keiyaki). In the middle ages in Japan and the rise of the tea ceremony and produced for the sake of shimizu. It got its name from burning it in front of kiyomizu temple. Shimizu is characterized by its elegant design and slim practices.

Besides being used as daily necessities, it is also one of the props often used in the tea ceremony. Favored by the royal family with a high aesthetic standard, shimizu is also one of the reasons why its artistic quality and quality continue to improve.

You can buy dishes, rice bowls, teacups and other baked goods from the local pottery shop near shimizu temple. It’s also a good idea to experience the fun of making pottery at the qingshuiyaotuoji workshop in the kilns’ gathering place (in the mountainous area of Kyoto).
